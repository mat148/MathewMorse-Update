GHG.

Aviatech is a web & marketing company that tasked us to create an immersive website based experience that would assist the high-end traveler in locating hotels that meet their requirements and then drives the users to the hotel's page to complete the sale. My solution to the problem was to create a website that was open, airy and used big beautiful images to draw the user in and help them find the perfect location to go on a vacation.

  • Client: GUI class project - Aviatech
  • Role: UI/UX Design
  • Timeline: 2013 ( 3 Months )

Setting goals.

Create a best in class online destination for select hotel research that promotes Online Direct Retailers through immersive visual experiences.

Project objective.

Project objectives were determined partially by the client as well as through class discussion.

1. Refined
2. Jet setter
3. Luxury virtual tool
4. Assisting travelers in locating hotels that meet their requirements
5. Google business photos

User research.

Since we had a client for this project our professor wanted us to go through the entire process of user research. We did user research and persona creation. Looking back on all the research now, I realize how "shallow" the research was. If I was doing this type of research now I would have done a much more in-depth analysis of everything.

Competitor research.

I researched two competitors and looked into info design and aesthetic design. Based on this research I determined five opportunities to innovate.

  • Open and airy experience
  • Present all information up front
  • Present all hotel images in a visually appealing and understandable manner
  • Emphasize the brands of the hotels
  • Work for both the person who knows where they want to go and the person who doesn’t

Hotwire.com

Aesthetic design.

Client defined user.

  • Looking for 4 - 5 star hotels
  • $500 - $1000 a night
  • Upscale customer
  • They have a couple places in mind
  • Jet Setter

User research.

We didn't have access to the ideal user which would be the high-end user. So instead we worked as a class to create questions about what users were looking for when they went on vacation. As well as what things they considered important when deciding which hotel to stay at. After which I conducted user research with friends and family.

How many nights a year users typically spend at a hotel?

Where do people go online when looking for hotels?

Most important thing when selecting a hotel?

Do you typically Select the same hotel when traveling? * Relevance by size

What information would influence your selection beyond your current brand? * Relevance by size

Does social media influence your vacation? * Relevance by size

When selecting a vacation destination what key aspect do you look for?

Defined user based on research.

  • Social Media is not a big factor
  • Brand oriented
  • Have places in mind when searching
  • Talks to family or friends for suggestions

UX design.

Taking all the research that was done in the research phase to inform personas, taxonomy maps, and wireframes.

Personas and user cases.

Based on the above research as well as client input I created personas and use cases of two different types of users, one who doesn't have a location in mind and another who does.

Scott - doesn't know where to go

A 56-year-old Lawyer who currently resides in Manhattan Penthouse with his wife of 28 years; June who is a 54-year-old Civil Engineer. He is tasked with finding a location for a romantic getaway but doesn’t know where to take her. He goes to Google and types in greatest hotels and at the top, there is the Greatest Hotels Group website. Clicks the link and is presented with a page full of beautiful imagery of featured hotels around the world and a search box at the top. He decides to search and filter out some things to narrow down his selection. He filters by atmosphere, Services, Scenery, and Food. He wants a location that has a romantic atmosphere, pampering services, beautiful scenery and fine dining. Scott finds the perfect place in Paris, France and he takes the tour through the hotel with google business photos and falls in love with it. He then shows it to June who agrees that it is wonderful so they click “buy now”.

Lauren - knows where she wants to go

A 36-year-old woman, who is a business executive. She is planning a trip to Las Vegas with her friends. So she goes on to GHG and searches for Las Vegas and is presented with a vertical list of pictures showing the location and hotel name. She clicks the more info button on the Hilton and sends the hotel info to her friends to suggest the hotel as a place to stay.
