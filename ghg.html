GHG.

Aviatech is a web & marketing company that tasked us to create an immersive website based experience that would assist high end traveler in locating hotels that meet their requirements and then drives the users to the hotels page to complete the sale. My solution to the problem was to create a website that was open, airy, and used big beautiful images to draw the user in and help them find the perfect location to go on a vacation.

  • Client: GUI class project - Aviatech
  • Role: UI/UX Design
  • Timeline: JAN - FEB 2013 ( 3 Months )

Create a best in class online destination for select hotel research that promotes ODR through immerse visual experiences.

UX design.

The user experience design of this project was largely focused on designing the interactions in the 3D space. I needed to make sure that everything was effortless and intuitive. As well as intergrating natural user interface interactions.

Sketching.

With my sketches I worked through the layout and structure of the app as well as some interactions in a low fidelity. Then I took the sketches to high fidelity so I could fully understand the structure.

Interaction design.

As I said above I was trying to make all interactions intuitive and effortless, as well as intergrating NUI interations.

Observe the 3D world by turning in place.

Move your phone towards the pillar you are looking at to move closer, pull your phone away to exit.

Walk around the pillar to observe the pillar.

Wave your phone back and fourth over the fire pillar as if you are putting out a fire to activate the pillar.

Blow on your phone to simulate wind on the air pillar to activate it.

Shake your phone to simulate a earth quake to activate the earth pillar.

Tip phone over as if you were pouring out water to activate the water pillar.

Drag the shoe to rotate in 3D.

Two finger drag apart to view the exploded view of the shoe.

Click on a piece of the shoe to see more information about that feature.
