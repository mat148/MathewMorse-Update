Get in touch!

Stories are the best way to get to know someone.

I’m a UX Designer born and raised in Syracuse, NY. From a very early age, I loved engineering and science, always building things, taking things apart, and inventing new things. At the same time, I was also creative, drawing, painting, and crafting Christmas presents for the family. I’ve always seen myself as both creative and technical, which makes sense as half of my family are artists and the other half are engineers.

I attended Rochester Institute of Technology for new media design. After which I moved out to Los Angeles to work at RED Interactive Agency as a designer, working with an amazing group of people on such projects as Pechanga.com, WGN America’s Outsiders and Underground Experience, as well as A&E’s Asterisk*.

After working with RED I transitioned to Freelance UI/UX as well as starting up two personal projects, a mobile app called Archive and a web app called Authora. Then in August of 2016, I headed to Accra, Ghana to work for a company called MindNET and start a seven-month-long journey through Ghana, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, and Malaysia where I decided to take my time and really learn about the people, culture, and history of each place. You can see more about my journey on the interactive experience I created for it Which is coming soon!.
