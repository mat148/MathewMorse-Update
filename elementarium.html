Elementarium.

What if there was a more engaging way to reach consumers that would increase brand awareness. Combining experimental browser-based 3D and Interaction Design to create a more engaging advertisement. There has been some exploration into VR advertisements with a VR headset such as oculus but that requires consumers to either own a VR headset or to go to a physical location. With my solution, I wanted to create a phone based VR that is accessible to everyone, wherever they are. As well as moving from a Graphical User Interface to a Natural User Interface.

  • Client: Experimental Senior Class Project
  • Role: UX Designer and three.js Developer
  • Timeline: 2014 ( 3 Months )

Setting goals.

The project goals were to rethink an interaction in terms of a particular UI problem or scenario, work through ideas and solutions that are radically different, and create something that fundamentally changes how we think about our interactions with contemporary technologies.

Project objective.

To achieve the project goal I created four objectives:
1. Research and be inspired by what currently exists.
2. Sketch interactions, ideas, and feelings using methods of layering and not erasing.
3. Design the workflow and interactions.
4. Finally, create an interactive prototype with Three.js.

User research.

Based on the project goals most of the research portion was focused on researching what we were interested in as well as what other people were doing in the experimental field. So for me, that meant researching WebGL, VR, AR, interfaces, installations, and interesting interactions.

Research.

In my research, I looked at multiple projects, some professional and some personal projects. From this research, I became interested in WebGL, VR, and using non-standard interactions.

UX design.

The user experience design of this project was largely focused on designing the interactions in the 3D space. I needed to make sure that everything was effortless and intuitive. As well as integrating natural user interface interactions.

Sketching.

With my sketches, I worked through the layout and structure of the app as well as some interactions in a low fidelity. Then I took the sketches to high fidelity so I could fully understand the structure.

Interaction design.

As I said above I was trying to make all interactions intuitive and effortless, as well as integrating NUI interactions.

Observe the 3D world by turning in place.

Move your phone towards the pillar you are looking at to move closer, pull your phone away to exit.

Walk around the pillar to observe the pillar.

Wave your phone back and forth over the fire pillar as if you are putting out a fire to activate the pillar.

Blow on your phone to simulate the wind on the air pillar to activate it.

Shake your phone to simulate an earthquake to activate the earth pillar.

Tip phone over as if you were pouring out water to activate the water pillar.

Drag the shoe to rotate in 3D.

Two fingers drag apart to view the exploded view of the shoe.

Click on a piece of the shoe to see more information about that feature.

Design.

In the design phase, I created a mood board to determine the look and feel of my environment. Created a mockup of the environment in C4D as well as creating and optimizing 3D elements for production.

Moodboard.

For this project, I created a mood board to display the design and feeling that I wanted the user to experience. While sketching I had an idea in my head of what I wanted the environment to look like as well as how I wanted it to feel. I wrote that I wanted the user to experience a sense of wonder and mystery.

Mockup.

Creating a quick mockup of the 3D environment so it would be easier to envision while I was developing the prototype. Also so that others could see the direction that I was heading.

Asset production.

Taking my mockup assets and turning them into final production ready assets.

Prototype creation.

Developing the prototype took the bulk of my time for this project as I have never worked with the Three.js library. So I knew that I was going to need a lot of time to experiment and research.

Development.

I created this prototype with Three.js, jquery, CSS, HTML, and the Three.js Editor. As well as a lot of help from articles, google, and developer friends.

Final thoughts.

I'm really excited to keep moving forward with this project. I learned quite a lot about Natural User Interfaces, Interactions, thinking deeply about design and the design workflow, as well as developing with WebGL. I had a lot of problems I had to overcome on both the design and development side but thinking through my problems made me a better designer and developer. My final takeaway is that I highly suggest everyone try something they have never done before, it might just turn out awesome.
